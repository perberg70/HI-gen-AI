Experience the synergy of human creativity and the power of AI. This course empowers secondary school educators to responsibly leverage Generative AI to enhance lesson planning, student engagement, and educational outcomes.

Why This Course, Why Now?

  • The rapid evolution of AI technologies is transforming education, with students increasingly using AI tools in their learning.
  • Secondary schools face growing pressure to prepare students for an AI-driven future while maintaining educational standards.
  • Evidence shows AI task speed-ups often need human review and process changes, so daily workload may not fall quickly (ILO, 2023; MIT CISR, 2024).
  • Teachers need practical skills to integrate AI responsibly into their classrooms and guide students in ethical AI usage.
  • This practice-rich course equips educators to navigate AI challenges and opportunities in secondary education.

Who Should Attend?

Ideal for secondary school educators who wish to deepen practical AI skills. No technical background required; teaching experience recommended.

Teachers

Enhance lesson planning, content creation, and student assessment with AI.

What You'll Learn & Achieve

Key Learning Outcomes:

  • Use GenAI safely & effectively for lesson planning, content creation, and student assessment.
  • Map AI opportunities & ethical considerations in secondary education contexts.
  • Design smart prompts for educational content and student engagement activities.
  • Create custom chatbots for educational purposes and deploy agents to automate tasks.
  • Guide students in responsible AI usage and digital literacy development.
  • Contribute to school AI policies and academic integrity guidelines.
  • A go-to place for profession-specific AI-related topics

Certification & Compliance:

  • Designed to ensure participants satisfy the EU AI Act conditions for responsible AI usage.
  • This course addresses educational AI ethics, student privacy, and responsible teaching practices.
  • Successful participants receive a Chalmers certified digital diploma.

What This Course Is Not

  • Tailor-made tutorials for every AI model: This course provides a general overview and practical strategies for using generative AI tools. We do not offer step-by-step guides for every specific AI model, as these tools are rapidly evolving.
  • Detailed programming or coding with AI: The course does not cover in-depth programming with AI, such as using AI in code editors (e.g., Cursor, Copilot, or other IDEs). No coding experience is required or expected.
  • How to build and understand Machine-Learning algorithms: This course does not teach the fundamentals of machine learning or how to develop your own ML algorithms from scratch.
  • Individual learning only: This is not only a self-paced course with no exchange with teachers or other learners. Instead we suggest you participate with peers and join our interactions.
