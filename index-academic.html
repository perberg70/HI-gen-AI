AI is quickly getting more capable. Join us to learn best practices and explore how to responsibly utilize AI to enhance research, teaching, and administrative processes. We will tackle obstacles, manage risks, and use AI to save time, improve quality and be able to do new things while maintaining academic integrity.

Why This Course, Why Now?

  • The rapid evolution of AI technologies presents significant challenges and opportunities for academic institutions.
  • Generative AI adoption in academia is accelerating, with universities increasingly expecting AI literacy from their staff.
  • Lack of AI competence is identified as a missed opportunity for higher education.

Who Should Attend?

Ideal for academic staff who wish to get confident in using AI. No specific prior skills are required.

Researchers

Explore research methodologies, find literature and improve academic writing with AI.

Teaching Staff

Integrate AI effectively into curriculum design and student assessment.

Administrative Staff

Streamline administrative processes and improve institutional efficiency.

What You'll Learn & Achieve

Key Learning Outcomes:

  • Use GenAI safely & effectively for research, teaching, and administrative tasks.
  • Map AI opportunities & ethical considerations in academic contexts.
  • Design smart prompts for literature reviews, science communication, and academic writing.
  • Create custom chatbots and deploy agents to automate tasks.
  • Be ready to navigate AI adoption in educational settings together with students and colleagues.
  • Locate and understand your institution's AI policies.

Certification & Compliance:

  • This course is designed to ensure participants satisfy the EU AI Act conditions for responsible AI usage.
  • Successful participants receive a Chalmers verifiable LinkedIn-ready badge and Chalmers certified digital diploma.

What This Course Is Not About

  • Tailor-made tutorials for every AI model: This course provides a general overview and practical strategies for using generative AI tools. We do not offer step-by-step guides for every specific AI model, as these tools are rapidly evolving.
  • Detailed programming or coding with AI: The course does not cover in-depth programming with AI, such as using AI in code editors (e.g., Cursor, Copilot, or other IDEs). No coding experience is required or expected.
  • How to build and understand Machine-Learning algorithms: This course does not teach the fundamentals of machine learning or how to develop your own ML algorithms from scratch.
